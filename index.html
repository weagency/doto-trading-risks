How to control your trading risks
If your goal is to succeed in trading, it’s key to improve your understanding of the market and follow basic money management principles.
These five tips can help you assess and control the risks of trading.
Open trade
Develop the right strategy
Your strategy should be in line with your financial goals, skill level, risk tolerance and trading style.
Don’t forget to set up stop loss and take profit levels so your trade closes at the right moment.
Explore the instruments you trade
Are you going to trade on oil?
Even a basic understanding of how this market works can give you useful insights into potential price movements.
Trade on multiple instruments
If you focus on a single instrument, you depend on its performance for success.
A well-diversified trading portfolio that combines instruments with low or negative correlation lets you mitigate potential risks.
Don’t use funds you cannot afford to lose
Your emergency fund and retirement savings should never be used for trading. The same applies to the money allocated for mortgage payments, education, and other necessities.
Educate yourself
Education is vital for a trader who seeks to identify and reduce risks related to financial markets.
Study technical and fundamental analyses to make your trading decisions more conscious.
